01 — The Opportunity

The 21 days are not a waiting room

Members have given blood. They are in an unusual psychological state: invested, curious, a little anxious. Results arrive in batches — we don't have the full picture on Day 1. We cannot control that. But we can control what the member experiences while they wait.

The 21-day window is an opportunity to build trust, grow their literacy around what the results will mean, and pull them into investment in their own health story. If we do it well, the final reveal becomes easier to receive — not just more anticipated.

There are practical constraints the strategy works within.

→
Results arrive in batches. The content strategy must be result-agnostic for approximately the first 15 days. Only after enough results land can we calibrate tone.
→
Data appetite is mixed. Some members want everything immediately. Others find detail overwhelming. Since we cannot segment yet, the default must work for both ends of that spectrum.
→
We are steering away from showing any data — and the reason matters. The moment we show a number or a result snippet, we invite a response. And once a member responds — worried, curious, or relieved — we enter a results conversation we are not yet equipped to manage well. The no-data boundary is not a content philosophy. It is a practical guardrail: no data means no thread to pull on. Reference the category only, never the number.
03 — The Framework

Three pillars. Every interaction earns its place through one of them.

Every concept in this system does one of three jobs: build trust (the member feels the doctor is closely involved and this is deeply personal), educate (the member gains vocabulary to receive the report), or engage (the member is pulled into active relationship with the product). If a concept does not clearly serve one of these, it is worth questioning whether it belongs.

Trust

The member feels the doctor is closely guiding this. It is personal. They are not alone in the process.

Interactions in this pillar
  • Progress Tracker (showcasing today)
  • Doctor-voice nudge (showcasing today)
  • Transparent results processing (showcasing today)
  • Personalised milestone acknowledgements (showcasing today)
Educate

The member gains the vocabulary and context to receive their report. They are not encountering a foreign language on Day 21.

Interactions in this pillar
  • The Literacy Engine (science primers)
  • Bite-size vocab builder
  • Biomarker explainers (e.g. biological age, VO₂ max)
  • Written in the doctor's voice, reusable across all members
Engage

The member is pulled into active participation — building investment in their own health story before the results arrive.

Interactions in this pillar
  • Intake Form (showcasing today)
  • The Reflection Prompt (single question, optional response)
  • The Behavioral Nudge (one actionable, universally applicable)
  • Future: preference & goal capture
On cadence: Timing is still being determined. But the principle is clear — intentional gaps matter. Days without messages should feel like calm, not absence. Content should earn its place in the sequence, not fill it.

02 — The Design Thinking

Core tensions that shaped the strategy

These are the competing pressures the design had to navigate. They are not agenda items — they are the rationale behind the framework that follows.

Build excitement
Something significant is coming. The data is real. This matters.
↔
Don't overpromise
You don't know if the result is good yet. Excitement can collapse into dread if the reveal is bad.
Keep them engaged
20 days of silence kills momentum and relationship.
↔
Don't exhaust them
HNIs have low tolerance for noise. One wrong message and they mute the thread.
Reveal early signals
Results arrive in batches. Sharing early builds anticipation.
↔
Don't leak the story
Early reveals without full context can cause disproportionate anxiety.
Educate and prepare
First-quarter members need vocabulary to receive the report.
↔
Don't signal the result
Explaining a biomarker the week before a bad result lands becomes a warning signal.
Feel human and personal
This is a health relationship. It must feel warm.
↔
It's fully automated
The doctor's voice is a brand layer. Inauthenticity risk is high.
04 — Today's Reveal

Two interactions. Shown in sequence.

Today we are looking at two interactions — the Intake Form and the Progress Tracker. The framework comes first, then the work.

Pillar: Engage
01 — The Intake Form

The Intake Form is the first moment the member actively participates. It is not a welcome questionnaire. By asking them to reflect on their goals, habits, and concerns across six conversational sections, we are building their investment in their own health story before any results arrive. They are co-authoring the narrative.

Positioned on Day 0–2 while motivation is highest. Six sections spread from Day 0 to Day 16 — each one unlocking a deeper layer of context that feeds into the final report.

What it does

Builds member investment. Captures context for personalisation. Makes the member feel their specifics are being woven into the clinical picture — not just a standard test.

A note on overlap

As the doctor gets richer context, it deepens the feeling of being personally guided — which also touches Trust. The primary job is Engage. The secondary resonance is real.

Pillar: Trust
02 — The Progress Tracker

The Progress Tracker gives the member visibility into where they are in the journey. It makes them feel like a participant, not a passive recipient. It signals that the doctor is present — that something is actively happening on their behalf — without revealing any results.

Two modes were explored: a static visual tracker that shows progress milestones clearly, and a voice note variation where the doctor narrates the progress moment. Both are presented as concepts. Today's conversation is about whether this concept belongs in the system, not which version is better.

What it does

Makes progress visible. Reduces anxiety. Builds a sense of shared journey between the member and the doctor-led system.

What it does NOT do

It does not show results. It does not reference numbers, categories, or any evaluation of the member's health. Categories only — never the number.

05 — Open Territory

What we're still figuring out

This strategy is a first showcase. The following are real open questions — we are naming them explicitly so the team knows what still needs to be resolved, and no one mistakes ambiguity for a decision.

Work in Progress
01
Cadence and sequencing. We know interactions need intentional gaps, and that silence should feel like calm. But the specific day-by-day rhythm — what goes when, how much spacing — is still being mapped. This will be built after pillar alignment is confirmed.
02
The grey zone: when a member replies to a message we send. The moment we reveal any test result or biocomposition data — even a partial reading, even a category — we open a thread we cannot fully manage. A member who sees their cholesterol is "elevated" or that their biological age marker is flagged will write back. They will ask questions we are not yet equipped to answer well in this window. The no-data boundary exists precisely to prevent this conversation from starting prematurely. But we still need a defined, warm protocol for when a member replies to any message we send — results-related or not. This needs to be built before we go live.
03
Defining what the right information looks like in this window. We know what we will not show — results, numbers, biocomposition data. What we are still shaping is the positive version of that: what types of information genuinely serve the member during the wait, build the right kind of anticipation, and prepare them to receive the report well. This is an active content design question, not a resolved one.
